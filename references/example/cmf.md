# Candidate Market Fit (CMF) — Maya Chen

> Fictional persona. Use as a shape reference, not as content.

## The one line
> Payments-infrastructure PM who ships systems engineering teams will depend on — issuing migrations, real-time authorization, evaluation harnesses for risk models.

## Conversation version (3 sentences)
> I build the payments infrastructure that other product teams sit on top of. The work I am known for is the kind where downtime, latency, or a bad rollout would be visible to every customer of the platform — issuer migrations, real-time auth, fraud-model gating. The proof point is the Beacon Pay BIN-sponsorship migration: 1.4M active cards moved in 11 months with zero reissuance and $3.2M annual fee reduction.

## Search-ready versions

**Standard:** Senior or Principal PM role on a payments-infrastructure team — issuing, acquiring, ledger, or risk platform — at a B2B fintech or an embedded-finance provider.

**Networking (1 sentence):** Looking for senior payments-infrastructure PM roles where the team owns auth, ledger, or risk.

**Best-fit title keywords:** Senior PM Payments Platform, Principal PM Issuing, Senior PM Risk Platform, Senior PM Embedded Finance, Senior PM Payments Infrastructure.

## Target scenarios

1. A B2B fintech with an in-house issuer-processor stack that needs to mature its developer surface and risk tooling.
2. An embedded-finance platform whose ledger or auth path is becoming the bottleneck on customer growth.
3. A bank or bank-tech company building a fintech-partner program that needs evaluation tooling, not more compliance review meetings.
4. A payments company replacing a third-party rules engine or scoring service with an in-house one and needing the rollout discipline that requires.

## Hard constraints

- Location / remote requirements: Remote-first or NYC hybrid. Will not relocate.
- Domain requirements: B2B payments, fintech infrastructure, or banking-tech. No consumer fintech apps. No crypto-only roles.
- Anti-patterns (what I will not consider): Pure roadmap-coordination PM roles; teams without engineering ownership of their stack; companies where "PM" reports into Sales or Marketing; firms in active turnaround under PE cost-cutting mandates.
