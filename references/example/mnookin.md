# Hard Constraints — Must-Haves and Must-Nots — Maya Chen

> Fictional persona. Use as a shape reference, not as content.

## Must-haves
A role must satisfy every item on this list to be worth pursuing.

- The team owns the stack it is responsible for — engineering, product, and risk in the same org, not vendored out.
- A real production system in scope. I am not interested in building yet another payments-platform pitch deck.
- A manager who has shipped infrastructure themselves. I will ask in the first interview.
- Access to production telemetry from week one. I cannot evaluate whether a system is working without it.
- Remote-first culture, or NYC hybrid with no more than 2 expected days on site.
- Compensation that reflects senior-IC infrastructure work in a high-cost metro. I will ask before second-round interviews.

## Must-nots
A single match here is enough to disqualify a role.

- "Strategic" PM roles where the actual work is roadmap aggregation across teams the PM does not own.
- Companies in active PE-led cost reduction. Glassdoor and recent layoff coverage are tells.
- Teams where Risk and Product report into different VPs and the relationship is described as "partnership" in the JD.
- Heavy SAFe / formal-ceremony cultures where outcome measurement is not part of the language.
- Roles that lead with "stakeholder management" as the top responsibility.

## Soft preferences
Nice-to-haves that influence ranking but do not disqualify.

- Founders or execs with a payments background — easier to make the case for infrastructure investment.
- A public engineering blog with recent technical posts. Signals a culture that documents.
- An existing internal evaluation framework (model gating, shadow traffic, etc.) — I would rather extend one than build from scratch.
